Signs and Symptoms of Breast Abnormalities:
    Lump: May be detected during a self-breast examination.
    Thickening of Breast Skin: Skin may feel firmer or appear dimpled.
    Nipple Discharge: Any unusual fluid from the nipple should be noted.
    Change in Breast Size or Firmness: One breast may appear larger, firmer, or differently shaped.
    Breast Pain: Persistent or unusual pain in the breast area.

Risk Factors for Breast Cancer:
    BRCA1 or BRCA2 Gene Mutation: Presence of these genetic mutations increases risk.
    Family History of BRCA Mutations: Having a first-degree relative (parent, brother, or sister) with BRCA1 or BRCA2 mutations, especially if genetic testing has not been done.
    Chest Radiation Before Age 30: Previous radiation therapy to the chest at a young age.
    Inherited Cancer Syndromes: Presence of Li-Fraumeni syndrome, Cowden syndrome, or Bannayan-Riley-Ruvalcaba syndrome, or having a first-degree relative with any of these syndromes.
    Other Genetic Mutations: ATM or TP53 gene mutations.
    Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT): Use of HRT during menopause.
